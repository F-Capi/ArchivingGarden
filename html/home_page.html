Archiving_Garden

Archiving Garden, a dynamic online platform dedicated to exploring the pivotal role of Internet archives in shaping culture and fostering democracy. Our initiative promotes ethical data practices while encouraging individuals to create their own archival projects within the digital garden. Here, you will find insightful narratives on Internet archives, unconventional archival inspirations, and practical guides for digitally preserving your work. Join us in reflecting on the profound impact of digital gardens and artificial intelligence on our lives, as we build a vibrant community around innovative archiving.

Internet Archive Founded

[1997]

Internet Archive Founded: Brewster Kahle establishes the Internet Archive, pioneering the effort to preserve digital content on a large scale. This becomes one of the first major initiatives to archive the web.

archive Numbers

It's challenging to provide an exact number of web archives currently on the World Wide Web because of the dynamic nature of the internet and the decentralized nature of web archiving efforts. There are numerous organizations, institutions, and projects dedicated to web archiving, each with its own collections and methodologies. Additionally, new web archives are continually being created, while others may become inactive or inaccessible over time.

NEW AGE

Archiving is essential in managing the growing accumulation of digitally-born materials. This process not only promotes digital literacy but also enhances the circulation of public and personal memories, contributing to research, educational value, and the preservation of cultural heritage.
