The Internet archive timeline

1997200120032005200620092012201720202023

Internet Archive Founded

[1997]

Internet Archive Founded: Brewster Kahle establishes the Internet Archive, pioneering the effort to preserve digital content on a large scale. This becomes one of the first major initiatives to archive the web.

launch of the "Wayback Machine”

[2001]

The Internet Archive introduces the “Wayback Machine”, allowing users to browse archived versions of web pages, capturing snapshots of the internet's evolution over time.

International Internet Preservation Consortium (IIPC) Formed

[2003]

Several national libraries and archives around the world come together to coordinate efforts on preserving internet content, recognizing the global importance of digital archiving.

First International Web Archiving Workshop

[2005]

Held in conjunction with the IIPC, this workshop brings together researchers and practitioners from around the world to discuss challenges and strategies in web archiving.

Archive-It Launched

[2006]

Internet Archive Founded: Brewster Kahle establishes the Internet Archive, pioneering the effort to preserve digital content on a large scale. This becomes one of the first major initiatives to archive the web.

Memento Project Starts

[2009]

A project aimed at making web archives more accessible and usable, introducing a time-travel approach to the web by integrating the past and present web through http protocol extensions.

UNESCO PERSIST Guidelines

[2012]

UNESCO PERSIST Guidelines for the Selection of Digital Heritage for Long-term Preservation: These guidelines are introduced, focusing on the preservation of digital heritage, including web content, to ensure ongoing access to cultural heritage.

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) Introduced

[2017]

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) Introduced: Although primarily a data protection regulation in the EU, GDPR impacts web archiving due to its provisions on personal data, prompting archives to consider privacy issues in their operations.

The lawsuit against the Internet Archive

[2020]

In 2020, the Internet Archive faced a lawsuit from four major publishers over its National Emergency Library, which lifted borrowing restrictions on digital books during the COVID-19 pandemic. The publishers claimed this constituted copyright infringement, sparking a debate on copyright law and access to information during crises.

launch of the "Wayforward Machine"

[2023]

"Wayforward Machine," an innovative project designed to envision the future of the internet. This initiative, inspired by its counterpart, the Wayback Machine, which archives past web pages, aims to stimulate discussion on the future challenges related to digital rights and information access. By projecting potential scenarios 25 years into the future, the Wayforward Machine encourages proactive thinking about the preservation of digital information and the ongoing fight for internet freedom.

archive Numbers

It's challenging to provide an exact number of web archives currently on the World Wide Web because of the dynamic nature of the internet and the decentralized nature of web archiving efforts. There are numerous organizations, institutions, and projects dedicated to web archiving, each with its own collections and methodologies. Additionally, new web archives are continually being created, while others may become inactive or inaccessible over time.
